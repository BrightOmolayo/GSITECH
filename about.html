About Us

Green-Shield Integrated Technologies G(GSI Tech) is an indigenous organization incorporated under the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) of Nigeria in conformity with the Companies and Allied Matters Act of 1990. It is a growing, dynamic and future-focused social enterprise consisting of developers and professionals who believe in creating strategies, systems, structures and solutions that are aimed at providing sustainable procedures for the specific development of people, businesses and communities. GSI Tech is a frontline implementer of the training tools developed under the USAID MARKETS II Project and consolidates values and capacity garnered through the project in its ongoing activities within and around the Niger Delta.

GSI Tech ensures that these measures are environmentally sustainable while maximizing the resources that the natural environment proffers, by the continual development of human capital and capacity. We introduce the deployment of apposite technologies or technological interventions, strategic coalition with experts with the needed professional characters and culture, and the use of relevant integrated development models to strengthen local economy, peoples' capacity to make wealth, and business chances of achieving their corporate objective

Our Objectives

Our thematic areas include:

  1. Agricultural business development services through enterprise training, mentorship and coaching. We are particularly into curriculum development and adaptation tailored to suit specific value chains and enterprises
  2. Technical capacity support aimed at strengthening local agricultural value chains.
  3. Cooperatives management (group dynamics and leadership courses etc)
  4. Facilitation of linkages to markets
  5. Project Related Investments

Our Mission

To maximize available material, human and agricultural resources, while at the same time aligning strategically with development partners who have the capacity to provide innovative technologies, resources and the expertise needed for improving targets in value chains.
