About Us

Green-Shield Integrated Technologies G(GSI Tech) is an indigenous organization incorporated under the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) of Nigeria in conformity with the Companies and Allied Matters Act of 1990. It is a growing, dynamic and future-focused social enterprise consisting of developers and professionals who believe in creating strategies, systems, structures and solutions that are aimed at providing sustainable procedures for the specific development of people, businesses and communities. GSI Tech is a frontline implementer of the training tools developed under the USAID MARKETS II Project and consolidates values and capacity garnered through the project in its ongoing activities within and around the Niger Delta.

Poultry

At GSI Tech, poultry is more than farming — it's a strategic tool for food security, income generation, and sustainable agriculture. We integrate modern poultry management practices with community training programs, helping local farmers scale production while maintaining biosecurity and environmental sustainability. Our team also leverages tech-driven monitoring to boost flock health, optimize feed use, and improve profitability.

Modern Cultivation

At the core of our planting programs is sustainability. We support communities with tailored seed selection, efficient irrigation systems, and organic soil enrichment strategies. By combining scientific research with on-the-ground experience, we ensure healthier crops, better harvests, and a greener future for rural agripreneurs.Our approach to farming combines indigenous knowledge with innovative solutions to empower smallholder farmers and agribusinesses. From crop planning and soil management to post-harvest handling, we provide end-to-end support. We use precision farming tools, climate-smart practices, and hands-on capacity-building to increase yields, preserve ecosystems, and create resilient food systems.

Produce Processing

We promote hygienic and efficient produce processing through modern processing techniques. Our improved kilns reduce harmful emissions, enhance product quality, and meet food safety standards — opening new markets for local livestock farmers. This initiative not only boosts income but also reduces post-harvest losses, preserving nutritional value and community livelihoods.
